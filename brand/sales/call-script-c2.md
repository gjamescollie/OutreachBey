# C2 — Discovery Call Script

**Target duration:** 20 minutes. You talk 5, they talk 10, demo takes 5.
**Goal:** Confirm the problem exists, run the live demo, get a verbal yes to receive a proposal.

---

## Before the Call

- Have the demo WhatsApp number ready to share (+1-242-[XXX-XXXX])
- Know their business type, tour/service price point, and review count from the target list
- Have Calendly open for booking the onboarding session if they say yes

---

## Opening (30 seconds — do not pitch yet)

> "Hey [Name], it's Granville — thanks for taking a few minutes. I'll be straight with you: I built a WhatsApp AI tool for Nassau businesses, and I'm talking to a handful of owners to see if it fits. Before I tell you anything about what it does — can I ask you one question? When you're [on the water / in the salon / showing a property / out on a job], what happens to the WhatsApp messages coming in?"

**Then stop talking. Let them answer.**

---

## Listen for the Problem

Common answers:
- "I try to check between [stops / sets / clients]"
- "My [wife / partner / staff member] handles it"
- "They just wait — I get to them when I can"
- "I lose bookings all the time, honestly"
- "It's a problem, yeah"

If they say "it's fine, we have someone on it" — ask: "How many hours a week does that person spend just on WhatsApp?" Then: "What does that cost you?" Most owners realize it's either expensive or unreliable when they say it out loud.

---

## Bridge — Quantify the Loss (60 seconds)

Customize the number to their price point:

> "That's exactly what I'm seeing. Here's the number that surprised me when I worked it out — at [$85 / $150 / $200] a booking, missing 2 inquiries a day while you're [running a trip / busy / away] is probably [$800–$1,200 / $1,500–$2,000] a week. That's not a phone problem — that's a staffing gap. And hiring someone just to watch WhatsApp doesn't make sense at this scale."

---

## Pivot to Live Demo (the pivot that closes deals)

> "I want to show you something rather than explain it. Can you open WhatsApp right now? Message +1-242-[XXX-XXXX] — just say 'Hi, how much is a tour?' and watch what comes back."

**Pause. Wait 10–15 seconds while they message it.**

> "That's what your customers would experience. It's running on a real Nassau [charter / business] persona right now — authentic voice, real pricing, real pickup info. The difference is it would be your number, your name, your pricing, your policies."

---

## Discovery Questions (capture answers for KB build)

After the demo lands:

> "Can I ask a few quick questions — this helps me understand if we're a fit and what setup would look like for you."

1. "What does your busiest inquiry period look like — cruise ship days? Weekends? A particular season?"
2. "What are the top 3 questions customers ask you before they book?"
3. "Do you have a booking link or do customers just WhatsApp you to reserve?"
4. "How many WhatsApp messages would you say you get on a busy day?"
5. "Is there a second number or person I should know about — like a partner who also uses the WhatsApp?"

---

## Transition to Proposal

> "Here's what setup looks like: we spend about an hour together going through your FAQs and pricing. My team builds the knowledge base and configures it around your business. We connect it to your WhatsApp number — you don't give me a password, it works like adding a second device. Goes live within a week.
>
> Setup is $2,500 one-time. Then $499 a month — no long contract, 30 days notice to cancel.
>
> If this makes sense, the next step is simple: I'll send you a one-page proposal today. You review it, we book 60 minutes for the setup session, and you're live within a week. Does that work?"

---

## Objection Handling

### "That's expensive."
> "I understand — it's a real number. Here's how I think about it: if Cay Receptionist recovers two missed bookings in your first month, you've covered the setup fee. Most operators I talk to are losing that in a single busy weekend. The monthly is less than a part-time employee to watch WhatsApp. Would it help to look at your numbers together?"

### "I need to think about it."
> "Of course. What's the main thing you'd want to think through — is it the price, the timing, or how it actually works on your number?" [Then address the specific thing. Don't disappear into "send me info."]

### "I'm not sure I trust AI to talk to my customers."
> "That's the most common thing I hear, and it's a fair concern. Here's the honest answer: the agent never pretends to be human, it never makes promises you haven't approved, and anything outside its knowledge gets flagged to you immediately. You reviewed the demo — did it say anything you wouldn't say yourself?" [Usually the answer is no.]

### "Can you do a discount?"
> "I keep pricing consistent across all clients — it's the only way the model works. But here's the framing I'd offer: you're not locked in. If you want to cancel after 30 days, 30 days notice and you're out. You're paying for one month plus setup to find out if it works. Most clients know by week two."

### "I already have someone handling it."
> "That's great — so you've already solved the staffing piece. The question is whether Cay Receptionist can handle the volume at a lower cost and free that person up for higher-value work. What does it cost you to have that person on WhatsApp right now?"

### "WhatsApp is working fine for us."
> "That's actually the best situation to be in — you've already validated that customers want to reach you on WhatsApp. The question is just about capacity: if your volume doubled tomorrow, could your current setup handle it without adding headcount? Cay Receptionist means you can scale the inquiries without scaling the labour."

---

## Close

If verbal yes:
> "Perfect. I'll send the proposal over today — give me a couple hours. Once you've had a chance to look it over, we book the setup session and you're live within a week. What's the best email to send it to?"

If maybe / soft interest:
> "No problem. I'll send the proposal and the one-pager so you have the full picture. If you have questions after reading it, just WhatsApp me — that's the easiest way to reach me. What's a good time this week for a follow-up if you want to talk it through?"
